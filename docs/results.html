Results

Results List

Results from running this algorithm can be downloaded from any of the links below. These files list the top-scoring (most relevant) articles that the algorithm found in the specified time periods.


Most fields in this document should be self-explanatory. If a field is empty, it’s because the information wasn’t available through the PubMed API.

Tracking Fields

Three fields are used for tracking model performance: score, label, and feedback.

The score field is the score assigned to the article by the model. Higher scores mean an article is more relevant. (This is the parameter that thresh is checked against in litmon.cli.rez.ResultsWriter.) The highest-scoring articles are included in each results file.

The label field is whether an article was identified by the people hired by the Methuselah Foundation as being relevant to the foundation’s mission. (I.e. the label is True iff the PMID of the article is included in data/pmids.txt).

The feedback field is a place for a manual reviewer to provide feedback on whether they think the article is relevant. We recommend scoring articles on a 0-2 basis (0=reject, 1=include, 2=super relevant). Non-integer scores are OK. In the future, we hope to expand this package to train on this feedback field in addition to the provided pmid list.

Baseline Comparison

Some of the results files include a Missed Articlces sheet, which lists all articles that were flagged by the people hired by the Methuselah Foundation, but were NOT identified by the model.

The model can be made to identify/include any of these articles by increasing the count variable or setting the thresh variable in litmon.cli.eval.ModelUser. Any significant change could lead to a much larger number of articles being identified/included in the results files.

Technical Details

For results from 2019/01 - 2021/12: Results were created through the config/std.yaml script, which used a model that was trained on data from 2013/01/01 through 2017/12/31 (YYYY/mm/dd). This model was then used to identify the top-scoring articles from each month of 2019-2021.

For results from 2022/01 and beyond: The cmd/live-pipeline script was used, which is triggered each month by the GitHub Actions .github/workflows/monthly.yaml.
